Your Skincare Routine

Simple, consistent, affordable. For melanin-rich skin that needs hydration, glow, and dark spot correction.

The Golden Rule of Melanin Skin

Hydration + SPF every single day. Dark spots, dullness, and uneven texture are almost always caused by dehydration and sun damage on dark skin. Fix those two things first — everything else is bonus.

Morning Routine
1
Gentle Cleanser
AM
Wash face with lukewarm water — not hot. Cleanses without stripping moisture.
Try: Neutrogena Hydro Boost Cleanser or CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser
2
Vitamin C Serum
AM
Fades dark spots, brightens skin, boosts collagen. Apply 2–3 drops, press into skin. Let dry 1 min.
Try: Garnier Vitamin C Serum or Drgina Vitamin C (affordable in Nigeria)
3
Moisturiser
AM
Seal in the serum. Look for hyaluronic acid or glycerin in ingredients.
Try: Neutrogena Hydro Boost Gel or simple shea butter mixed with a few drops of water
4
SPF 30–50 Sunscreen ☀️
AM — NON-NEGOTIABLE
The single most important step for dark skin. Sun exposure worsens dark spots and causes premature ageing on melanin skin. Wear it even indoors near windows.
Try: Black Girl Sunscreen, Neutrogena Ultra Sheer SPF50, or La Roche-Posay (Miniso/online)
Evening Routine
1
Double Cleanse (if wearing makeup)
PM
Micellar water first to remove makeup, then gentle cleanser. If no makeup — just cleanser.
2
Niacinamide Serum
PM
Targets dark spots, minimises pores, evens skin tone, controls oil. This is your texture corrector.
Try: The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc (widely available in Nigeria)
3
Heavy Night Moisturiser
PM
Skin repairs itself overnight — give it rich hydration to work with. This is when you get supple skin.
Raw shea butter + 2 drops rosehip oil (affordable, natural, incredibly effective for dark skin)
4
Lip Balm
PM
Apply a thick layer before bed. Your lips heal overnight. Vaseline works perfectly.
Weekly Treatments
1
Exfoliate (1–2x per week)
Weekly
Removes dead skin, allows serums to penetrate deeper. Use a gentle chemical exfoliant — NOT harsh scrubs.
Try: The Ordinary AHA/BHA Peeling Solution or a simple sugar + honey scrub
2
Face Mask (1x per week)
Weekly
Multani mitti (Fuller's Earth) + rose water mask is excellent for dark skin — brightens and deep cleans.
Affordable at any Nigerian pharmacy or market
3
Gua Sha or Face Massage
2–3x week
Reduces puffiness, lifts the face, improves circulation and that natural glow. Do it while applying night moisturiser.
For your lips specifically: Mix shea butter + a tiny pinch of sugar, scrub gently once a week. Follow with vaseline every night. Soft, full lips in 2 weeks.
Drink water first: Supple skin starts from inside. 2–3 litres a day. Your serum cannot hydrate dehydrated skin from within — water does that.

Body Skin Care

Head to toe supple, even, glowing skin. Not just your face.

Your Body Skin Goals

Even skin tone, soft texture, no ashy patches, no dark knees or elbows, smooth legs, and an overall healthy glow from neck to feet.

Shower Routine
1
Use Lukewarm Water Only
Hot water strips natural oils from skin. Lukewarm water is the minimum change that improves dryness and dullness immediately.
2
Body Scrub (2–3x per week)
Exfoliate in the shower to remove dead skin. This is what creates that lit-from-within glow.
DIY: Brown sugar + coconut oil + lemon juice. Or: St. Ives Apricot Scrub
3
Moisturise IMMEDIATELY after shower
While skin is still slightly damp — this locks in moisture. Don't wait until you're fully dry. This one habit changes everything.
Shea butter + a few drops of body oil (coconut, almond, or jojoba)
Problem Areas
🦵
Dark Knees & Elbows
Apply lemon juice + shea butter nightly. Exfoliate 2x/week. Results in 3–4 weeks.
✨
Ashy Skin
Moisturise within 2 mins of shower every single day. No exceptions.
🍑
Inner Thighs / Dark Spots
Niacinamide body lotion + loose clothing. Reduce friction. Takes 6–8 weeks.
👣
Feet & Heels
Soak weekly, pumice stone, then thick vaseline + socks overnight. Soft feet in 2 weeks.
Weekly Body Ritual
1
Full Body Scrub Sesh
Once a week — scrub from neck to feet in the shower. Focus on knees, elbows, ankles, and back of arms.
2
Foot Soak + Care
Warm water + salt + lemon for 15 min. Scrub with pumice. Pat dry. Apply thick shea or vaseline + wear socks to bed.
3
Body Oil Treatment
Massage body oil all over after scrubbing — especially on stretch marks, dry patches, and dark areas. Rosehip oil for dark spots.
The #1 body skin rule: Moisturise damp skin every single day. This alone — done consistently — will give you supple, glowing skin within 3–4 weeks. Everything else is extra.

Hair Care

Healthy, moisturised natural hair + neat edges = 50% of looking put together done already.

Your Hair Priorities

From the photo — your edges need moisture and consistent care, and your natural hair needs a hydration routine. The goal is healthy, defined, and always neat — not necessarily long or elaborate.

The LOC Method (For Natural Hair)
L
Liquid — Water or leave-in conditioner
Always moisturise natural hair with water first. Spray bottle with water is your best friend.
O
Oil — Seal the moisture in
Apply a light oil over the water — coconut, jojoba, or castor oil. Seals moisture so it doesn't evaporate.
C
Cream — Shea butter or hair cream
Apply a thick cream or butter last to lock everything in. This keeps your hair moisturised for days.
Edge Care Routine
1
Apply castor oil to edges nightly
Jamaican Black Castor Oil (JBCO) is the gold standard for edge growth and thickness. Massage in gently — don't rub aggressively.
2
Lay edges with a soft brush + edge control
Use a soft toothbrush or edge brush. Don't use hard gels that cause breakage. Less tension = more growth.
Try: Eco Styler Gel (olive oil) or Got2b Ultra Glued lightly
3
Satin scarf/bonnet at night
Non-negotiable. Cotton pillowcases absorb moisture and break edges. Satin or silk only.
Weekly Wash Day
1
Pre-poo (optional but powerful)
Apply coconut or olive oil to hair 30 min before washing. Prevents protein loss during shampoo.
2
Shampoo — Sulphate Free
Regular shampoo strips natural hair. Use sulphate-free only.
Try: SheaMoisture or As I Am shampoo
3
Deep Condition — 20–30 min
This is the most important wash day step. Apply deep conditioner, cover with a plastic cap, leave for 20–30 min. Transforms dry hair.
Try: ORS Olive Oil Replenishing Pack or Shea Moisture Manuka Honey Masque
4
Style with LOC Method
After washing, apply leave-in → oil → cream while hair is still wet. Then style as desired.
Neat = put together: A simple stretched bun, low puff, or braided style that's fresh and moisturised will always look more polished than elaborate styling on dry, neglected hair.

Looking Put Together

Always neat, always intentional. This isn't about expensive clothes — it's about fit, colour, and grooming.

"Put Together" is 80% Grooming, 20% Clothes

Clean skin, fresh hair, ironed clothes, and a confident posture will make a ₦3,000 outfit look like ₦30,000. Start there before buying anything new.

Your Signature Style Formula
1
Fit Over Fashion
Clothes that fit your body perfectly always look expensive. One well-fitting piece beats 5 ill-fitting ones. Tailor what you have if needed — a seamstress is cheap in Nigeria.
2
Build a Neutral Base
Invest in: white tees, black trousers, nude/beige pieces, a good-fitting jeans, and one blazer. Everything else is accent.
3
Add Your Colour Accent
As a dark-skinned woman, you look incredible in: earth tones (rust, terracotta, burnt orange), deep jewel tones (emerald, burgundy, cobalt), and bright whites and creams. Lean into these.
4
One Statement Piece Per Outfit
Statement earrings, a bold bag, or a standout top — then keep everything else simple. Trying to do too much at once looks cluttered, not stylish.
Outfit Formulas by Occasion
🏢 Office / Work Days
Well-fitted trousers + tucked blouse + flat mules or block heels
Keep it clean and structured. One piece of jewellery. Hair neat and moisturised. SPF on the face.
🏠 Casual / Home / Errands
Good-fitting jeans + simple tee or bodysuit + clean sneakers
Even casual should look intentional. Iron your tee. Moisturise your skin. Do your edges.
🌙 Evening / Going Out
Midi dress or co-ord set in a rich tone + heels + minimal jewellery
You don't need to show a lot of skin to look stunning. Coverage with elegance reads wealth.
🙏 Sunday / Events
Ankara or monochrome set + statement earrings + heels
Lean into your culture. Ankara on a well-fitted silhouette is always elite.
Non-Negotiable Grooming Habits
💅
Nails
Clean, shaped nails always. Even bare nails — keep them filed and moisturise your cuticles.
👄
Lips
Moisturised lips, always. A simple tinted lip balm elevates your whole face.
🦷
Teeth
Brush twice daily. Use charcoal or whitening toothpaste once a week. A bright smile is magnetic.
👃
Fragrance
A signature scent makes you unforgettable. Apply to pulse points — wrists, neck, behind knees.
Colours that make dark skin glow: Rust, terracotta, cobalt blue, emerald green, mustard yellow, burgundy, bright white, and champagne gold. These are your power colours.

Feet Care Routine

Polished from head to toe. Soft, smooth, healthy feet complete your glow-up and make you feel confident.

Your Feet Goals

Soft, smooth heels with no cracks or ashy patches. Trimmed nails with healthy cuticles. Feet that feel and look polished — because a woman who is glowing takes care of every inch of her body.

Daily Feet Care
1
Moisturise Feet Immediately After Shower
While skin is still slightly damp, apply thick moisturiser to feet, paying special attention to heels and the sides of your toes. Don't skip this — it prevents cracks and keeps skin soft.
Shea butter + a drop of coconut oil, or Vaseline
2
Evening Feet Treatment
Before bed, apply a thick layer of moisturiser (or vaseline) to feet, put on soft socks, and sleep. Your feet repair and soften overnight. Do this 3–4 times a week minimum.
Vaseline + cotton socks
3
Keep Toe Nails Trimmed & Shaped
Trim every 3–4 weeks. Use nail clippers, not scissors. File into a rounded or slightly squared shape. Keep edges smooth to prevent breakage.
4
Moisturise Cuticles
Push back cuticles gently with a cuticle pusher 2x per week. Apply cuticle oil or a small amount of castor oil. Healthy nails start with healthy cuticles.
Weekly Deep Feet Soak
1
Soak in Warm Water (15–20 min)
Warm water + Epsom salt (or sea salt) + a squeeze of lemon juice. This softens dead skin, relaxes tired feet, and prepares them for exfoliation. Soak every Sunday or as needed.
2
Exfoliate with Pumice Stone
After soaking, gently rub heels and any rough patches with a pumice stone. Use light pressure — don't be aggressive. Focus on heels first, then balls of feet and sides.
3
Scrub with a Foot Scrub (Optional)
Use a gentle foot scrub or DIY: ground oatmeal + honey + a few drops of lemon juice. Scrub gently in circular motions. This removes dead skin and leaves feet smooth.
4
Rinse & Pat Dry Completely
Always dry feet thoroughly, especially between the toes. Moisture left behind causes fungal infections and odour.
5
Deep Moisturise + Wear Socks
Apply thick shea butter or vaseline to entire foot (including between toes and cuticles). Put on soft cotton socks and leave on for 30 min to 2 hours, or overnight for best results.
Problem Areas Fix
🩹
Cracked Heels
Exfoliate + moisturise nightly with vaseline + socks. In 2–3 weeks, even severe cracks heal. Consistency is key.
⚪
Ashy Feet
Exfoliate weekly and moisturise immediately after shower, while skin is damp. Darkness often comes from dryness — fix that first.
🔴
Dark/Discoloured Skin
Apply lemon juice mixed with shea butter nightly. Lemon is a natural lightener. Results in 4–6 weeks with consistency.
🧅
Calluses
Soak, pumice, then moisturise. If too thick, see a pedicurist. Never cut calluses yourself — you risk injury and infection.
Monthly Pedicure (At Home or Salon)
1
Full Soak & Deep Exfoliation
A thorough soak followed by pumice or a professional foot file. This resets your feet and keeps them in top condition.
2
Nail Care & Shaping
Trim, file, and shape nails. Basic maintenance keeps them looking intentional and polished.
3
Polish or Keep Natural
Up to you — a simple neutral polish or bare, healthy nails always look polished. If using polish, use a good base coat to prevent staining.
4
Moisturise & Massage
Finish with a rich moisturiser and give yourself a 5-minute foot massage. Reflexology improves circulation and helps you feel energised.
Soft feet in 2 weeks guaranteed: Exfoliate weekly, moisturise immediately after shower on damp skin, and apply vaseline + socks every other night before bed. That's it. This combination is non-negotiable for polished feet.
Prevent foot odour & fungus: Always dry feet completely after bathing (especially between toes), change socks if they get damp, and wear breathable footwear when possible. Keep feet dry and they'll stay healthy.

Healthy Eating Blueprint

Intermittent fasting, balanced nutrition, and staying off processed sugar for glowing skin, stable energy, and a fit body.

Why Eating Matters for Your Glow

Your skin reflects what you eat. Sugar causes breakouts and inflammation. A healthy eating routine with stable blood sugar gives you clear skin, energy all day, stable weight, and that lit-from-within glow. This isn't about restriction — it's about eating intentionally.

Intermittent Fasting: Eat Between 2–6pm
Why
Benefits of IF (Intermittent Fasting)
Reduces bloating, improves digestion, stabilises blood sugar, boosts metabolism, reduces cravings for junk food, and gives your skin time to repair during fasting hours. You'll have clearer skin and more consistent energy.
How
Your Eating Window: 2pm–6pm
This is relatively easy to sustain. Morning: green tea, water, black coffee (optional). Afternoon 2–6pm: eat 2–3 balanced meals. After 6pm: only fruits, herbal tea, or light broth-based pepper soup if hungry.
Flexibility
You Don't Have to Do IF Every Day
Start with 3–4 days a week if every day feels too hard. Your body will adapt. Even 2 days a week gives you skin clarity and weight management benefits. Find what works for your body.
Morning Routine (Before 2pm Eating Window)
1
Green Tea Upon Waking
Upon Waking
Warm water with green tea or just hot water with lemon. Boosts metabolism, aids digestion, and is rich in antioxidants for glowing skin. No sugar — drink it plain or with a tiny bit of honey if needed.
Green tea, chamomile, or ginger tea
2
Drink Plenty of Water
Morning
At least 500ml of water first thing. You're breaking a 12+ hour fast. Water hydrates your skin from within and aids digestion throughout the day.
3
Stay Busy Until 2pm
Work, exercise, or activities keep your mind off hunger. By 2pm your first meal will feel satisfying, and you'll avoid mindless snacking.

(2–3pm)
Balanced Protein + Carbs + Veggies
Examples:

Snack (4pm)
Fruit or Light Snack

Evening Meal

Lighter, Balanced Dinner
Examples:

Eat by 6pm to give your stomach 12+ hours rest overnight.
After 6pm Guidelines
Drink
Herbal Tea or Water Only
Green tea, chamomile, ginger tea, or plain water. Keep hydrating. Your skin repairs at night — water supports this process.
If Hungry
After 6pm
Fruits or Light Pepper Soup
Acceptable options:
• Fresh fruits: watermelon, pineapple, orange, pawpaw
• Light pepper soup (no garri/carbs — just the broth and vegetables)
• Herbal tea with a piece of fruit

Avoid: rice, bread, heavy carbs, or sugar after 6pm.
Sleep
10–11pm
Fasting Overnight (12–14 hours)
By skipping breakfast and eating only between 2–6pm, you give your digestive system a long rest. This reduces bloating, improves gut health, and boosts clarity and energy. Your body does its best repair work in this fasted state.
Foods to Add Regularly
🥬
Vegetables
Broccoli, carrots, spinach, peppers, tomatoes. Add to every meal. They're high in nutrients, low in calories, and flush out toxins for clear skin.
🍌
Fruits
Bananas, oranges, pineapple, watermelon, pawpaw. Eat during eating window or after 6pm. Rich in vitamins for glowing skin.
🥚
Protein
Eggs, fish, chicken, lean beef, beans. Keep muscles strong, support skin health, and keep you full longer.
🌾
Whole Grains
Brown rice, oats, whole wheat bread. Better for digestion and energy than white carbs. Oats are especially good for morning meals.
Breakfast Alternatives (On Non-IF Days)
1
Oats + Fruit
Simple: oats + hot water/milk + sliced banana or berries + a drizzle of honey (skip sugar). Takes 5 minutes, keeps you full until lunch, and is amazing for digestion and skin clarity.
2
Banana Oat Pancakes
Recipe (2 servings):
1. Blend 2 bananas + 1/2 cup oats + 1 egg + pinch of cinnamon
2. Pour onto a non-stick pan
3. Cook like pancakes (2–3 min each side)
4. Top with fresh fruit or a tiny drizzle of honey

Naturally sweet, no processed sugar, packed with fibre and protein. Makes you feel full until 2pm.
3
Scrambled Eggs + Whole Grain Toast + Tomato
Protein-rich, simple, satisfying. Keeps blood sugar stable and prevents mid-morning energy crashes that lead to sugar cravings.
Sugar Elimination (Your Secret Weapon)
What Sugar
Does
Causes Breakouts, Bloating, Weight Gain, Energy Crashes
Sugar spikes insulin, triggers inflammation in your skin (causing breakouts), feeds bad gut bacteria, and leads to cravings that keep you stuck. Staying off it is one of the fastest ways to clear skin and feel energised.
Foods to
Avoid
Processed Sugar & Hidden Sugars
Obvious: Soft drinks, sweets, cakes, biscuits, pastries, ice cream
Hidden sugar (read labels!): Packaged fruit juice, flavoured yogurt, breakfast cereals, sweet bread, ketchup, sauce mixes

When you want something sweet: eat fruit, have a tiny piece of dark chocolate (70%+ cacao), or drink tea with honey.
First 2
Weeks
After 2 Weeks, Your Cravings Drop Significantly
Your taste buds reset. After 3 weeks, a regular sweet snack will taste too sweet. By week 4, you won't even want it. Push through the first 2 weeks — the payoff in clear skin and energy is worth it.
Start simple: You don't have to do IF every day. Start with 2–3 days a week, add vegetables to all your meals, swap sugar for fruit, and drink green tea in the morning. These alone will clear your skin and give you stable energy within 2–3 weeks.
Hydration is key: 2–3 litres of water daily (more if you're doing IF). Water supports digestion, flushes out toxins, and is the foundation of glowing skin. Make it your priority.
